<h1>Stop Website Pop-ups & Alerts</h1> 
<p id="googlefont">Constant pings, sliding notifications, and intrusive pop-ups can make it impossible to focus. Most of this "noise" is caused by websites asking for permission to send you alerts that you don't actually need.

This guide shows you how to take back control of your screen and disable annoying digital distractions for good.</p>

<img style="width: 100%;" src="images/notification_block_visual.png" alt="Graphic showing a blocked notification bell">
<div>
    <h2><br><b>Where the Noise Comes From</b></h2>
    <p>Distractions usually enter your workspace through three main channels: </p>
    <ul>
        <li><p><b>Browser Notifications</b>: Websites that "pushed" permission requests while you were browsing.</p></li>
        <li><p><b>System Alerts</b>: Windows or Mac suggestions and news feeds built into the taskbar.</p></li>
        <li><p><b>Startup Pop-ups</b>: Apps that launch and immediately show "What's New" or "Buy Now" windows.</p></li>
    </ul>
</div>

<div>
    <h2><br><b>How to Block the Distractions</b></h2>
    <p>Use these settings to clean up your interface: </p>
    <ol>
        <li><p><b>Browser Lockdown</b>: Go to Settings > Privacy > Site Settings and toggle "Notifications" to blocked.</p></li>
        <li><p><b>Focus Mode</b>: Enable "Focus Assist" (Windows) or "Do Not Disturb" (Mac) to silience all alerts instantly.</p></li>
        <li><p><b>Clean the Taskbar</b>: Right-click your taskbar to turn off "News and Interests" or "Search Highlights."</p></li>
    </ol>
</div>

<div id="imagedivider">   
    <img id="content_image" src="images/clean_desktop_icon.png" alt="Illustration of a clean, distraction-free computer screen">
    <p>A quiet computer is a productive computer. Clean your settings once a month to keep it that way.</p>
</div>

<div>
    <h2><br><b>Conclusion</b></h2>
    <p>Setting boundaries for your browser and operating system is the best way to reduce digital fatigue. Once you’ve silenced the unnecessary notifications and cleared out the taskbar clutter, you’ll find it much easier to stay focused on the task at hand. Remember to check your "Notification Settings" whenever you install a new app to keep your desktop quiet and your workspace productive.</p>
</div>
