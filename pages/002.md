<h1>Fixing a Slow Internet Connection</h1> 
<p id="googlefont">A lagging connection is one of the most frustrating digital hurdles. Whether your videos are buffering or pages are taking forever to load, the issue usually stems from a few common "clogged" areas in your system.

In this guide, we will walk through the immediate steps to clear the digital pipes and get your speed back to normal.</p>

<img style="width: 100%;" src="images/speed_test_visual.png" alt="Graphic showing high speed internet gauges">
<div>
    <h2><br><b>Common Speed Killers</b></h2>
    <p>Before buying a new router, check if these common issues are draining your bandwidth: </p>
    <ul>
        <li><p><b>Background Apps</b>: Programs like OneDrive or Steam downloading updates in the background.</p></li>
        <li><p><b>Browser Cache</b>: Old website data stored in your browser that has become corrupted or heavy.</p></li>
        <li><p><b>Signal Interference</b>: Physical objects or other electronics blocking your Wi-Fi signal.</p></li>
        <li><p><b>Too Many Tabs</b>: Each open tab uses a small amount of data and processing power to stay active.</p></li>
    </ul>
</div>

<div>
    <h2><br><b>Step-by-Step Troubleshooting</b></h2>
    <p>Follow these steps to identify the bottleneck: </p>
    <ol>
        <li><p><b>Run a Speed Test</b>: Use a site like Speedtest.net to see if you are actually getting the speeds you pay for.</p></li>
        <li><p><b>Clear Browser Data</b>: Go to your browser settings and "Clear Cache and Cookies" to remove temporary junk.</p></li>
        <li><p><b>The 30-Second Reset</b>: Unplug your router, wait 30 seconds, and plug it back in to clear its internal memory.</p></li>
    </ol>
</div>

<div id="imagedivider">   
    <img id="content_image" src="images/router_reset_icon.png" alt="Illustration of a router being reset">
    <p>Resetting your hardware is often the fastest fix for 90% of connectivity issues.</p>
</div>

<div>
    <h2><br><b>Conclusion</b></h2>
    <p>Most internet speed issues aren't caused by a major service failure, but rather by a "clogged" home network. By staying on top of background app usage and giving your router a quick reset every once in a while, you can keep your connection running at the speed you actually pay for. If you’ve followed these steps and things still feel sluggish, it might be time to contact your provider to check the line coming into your home.</p>
</div>
